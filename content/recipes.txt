<h3>Baked Potatoes</h3>
<ol>
<li>If using a conventional oven, preheat to 350°F.</li>
<li>Scrub one (or more) large potato(es).</li>
<li>Use a fork to poke a fair number of holes in the potato so that it doesn't explode.</li>
<li>Optionally coat the potato with oil and salt.</li>
<li>Bake for about an hour or microwave until done (time depends on microwave power).</li>
<li>Make a lengthwise incision deep into one side of the potato and squeeze so that it opens.</li>
<li>Top with salt, pepper, sour cream, cheese, bacon, and/or any other preferred toppings.</li>
</ol>

<h3>Boiled Potatoes</h3>
<ol>
<li>Scrub several small to medium potatoes.</li>
<li>Cut into bite-sized pieces.</li>
<li>Put the pieces into an appropriately-sized pot and more than cover them with water.</li>
<li>Put them on the stove and apply maximum heat.</li>
<li>Turn down the heat as necessary to prevent boiling over.</li>
<li>Remove from heat when the desired consistency has been acheived.</li>
<li>Drain water from pot.</li>
</ol>

<h3>Mashed Potatoes</h3>
<ol>
<li>Make boiled potatoes.</li>
<li>Add a pat of butter and a large splash of milk.</li>
<li>Add salt, pepper, and any other desired seasonings.</li>
<li>Mash well.</li>
</ol>

<h3>Poutine</h3>
<ol>
<li>Make french fries.</li>
<li>While still hot, add a fairly thick but light gravy and cheese curds.</li>
<li>Serve immediately.</li>
</ol>
